## Key decisions in plain English

**Apache 2.0 over GPL-3.0 / ELv2.** We compete with Helicone (GPL-3.0
viral) and Phoenix (ELv2 SaaS-blocked) by being the OSS that doesn't
make customers nervous. Reference patterns; never fork.

**Rust gateway, not Go.** Low p95 overhead with full tracing on is a
hot-path budget (measured figures publish with the Reliability Benchmark v1.0).
Rust's zero-allocation request flow and tokio's runtime get us there without
exotic tuning.

**ClickHouse hot tier, R2 cold tier.** High-cardinality, high-throughput OLAP
requirements rule out Postgres for spans. ClickHouse is the cheapest path to
the throughput.

**Sigstore Rekor for audit anchoring.** Tamper evidence without us
having to run a notary. Public log, free, audited, and standard.

**OTLP semconv compatibility.** Free interop with the entire OTel
ecosystem. Customers who already export OTel traces don't have to swap
SDKs — they swap exporters.

**Agent-tool safety inline, not async.** The point is to catch a
compromised or hallucinated tool call at the gateway, inline, rather than
reconstruct it from an incident after the fact. The budget is real
(`combined, inline`) and `benchmark-runner` enforces it.

**Evals are the merge gate.** A claim that a guard exists is gated on
its eval being green on `main`; a red eval blocks the merge that would
ship the claim.
